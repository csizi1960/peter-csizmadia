Particles in this simulation:
  1. Initial, elementary particles: u, d, s quarks and their corresponding antiquarks. The color of a quark is red, green, or blue, each with 1/3 probabability.
  2. Intermediate composite particles: diquarks and antidiquarks. The color of a diquark is antired (green+blue), antigreen (red+blue), or antiblue (red+green), each with 1/3 probabability.
  3. Final (colorless) composite particles: mesons, baryons, antibaryons.
Each coalescence process has a weight, proportional to its coalescence factor.
  • Cqq: coalescence factor of two light quarks (u, d), two light antiquarks, or a light quark and a light antiquark
  • Cqs: a light and a strange quark, ...
  • Css: two strange quarks, ...
  • Cqqq: uu, ud, or dd light diquark and a light quark, ...
  • Cqqs: uu, ud, or dd light diquark and a strange quark, ...
  • Cqsq: us or ds diquark and a light quark, ...
  • Cqss: us or ds diquark and a strange quark, ...
  • Cssq: ss diquark and a light quark, ...
  • Csss: ss diquark and a strange quark, ...
Diquarks may decay into two quarks. The weight of the decay process is proportional to the coalescence factor of the creation of a diquark of the same type.

At each step in the simulation, a coalescence or (diquark) decay process is chosen randomly, according to its weight.
