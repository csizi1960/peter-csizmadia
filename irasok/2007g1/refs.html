H. J. Fowler and D. R. Archer, "Conflicting Signals of Climatic Change in the Upper Indus Basin", Journal of Climate 19 (2006) 17, pp. 4276–4293.
"Temperature data for seven instrumental records in the Karakoram and Hindu Kush Mountains of the Upper Indus Basin (UIB) have been analyzed for seasonal and annual trends over the period 1961–2000 and compared with neighboring mountain regions and the Indian subcontinent. ... Winter mean and maximum temperature show significant increases while mean and minimum summer temperatures show consistent decline. ... Decreases of ~20% in summer runoff in the rivers Hunza and Shyok are estimated to have resulted from the observed 1°C fall in mean summer temperature since 1961, with even greater reductions in spring months. The observed downward trend in summer temperature and runoff is consistent with the observed thickening and expansion of Karakoram glaciers, in contrast to widespread decay and retreat in the eastern Himalayas."

C. Mayer, A. Lambrecht, M. Belò, C. Smiraglia, G. Diolaiuti, "Glaciological characteristics of the ablation zone of Baltoro glacier, Karakoram, Pakistan", Annals of Glaciology 43 (2006) 1, pp. 123-131.
"From the comparison of historical measurements and photographs it is shown that the snout of Baltoro glacier is oscillating back and forth a couple of hundred metres. Otherwise it seems not to react with the same magnitude as other glaciers to the climatic change. Elevation changes at Concordia are a few tens of metres at the most."

L. Iturrizaga, "Historical and recent glacier variations in the Karakoram Mountains", Geophysical Research Abstracts 9 (2007) 05470.
"Even though a considerable part of the Karakoram glaciers have shown signs of glacier retreat, most of the longer glaciers (> 45 km), such as the Baltoro, Batura, Khurdopin glaciers have been rather stagnant in the last century. ... However, many glaciers have mainly shrinked by downwasting rather than by icefrontal recession. At the same time a lot of glaciers, in particular the purely avalanche- fed glaciers, have shown considerable high amounts of vertical changes of the glacier surface. ... It might be assumed that especially the longer Karakoram glaciers will react delayed to a global temperature warming."

M. P. Bishop, J. F. Shroder, "Field and Satellite Observations of Supra-Glacial Lake Fluctuations on the Baltoro Glacier, Karakoram Himalaya, Pakistan", American Geophysical Union, Fall Meeting 2005.
"Image analysis results show a significant increase in the frequency and size of supraglacial lakes from 2000 to 2004. These results support field observations that indicate that the Baltoro glacier is rapidly downwasting. Break-out flooding also explains why the glacier does not exhibit any terminal moraines."
